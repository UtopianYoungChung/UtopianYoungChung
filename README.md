## About Me

I am a PhD student at the Faculty of Information, University of Toronto (2025–2029), supervised by Professor Eric Yu. My work sits at the intersection of conceptual modeling, requirements engineering, and the organizational impact of AI. I build i\* (iStar) Strategic Dependency (SD) and Strategic Rationale (SR) models to surface the intentions, dependencies, vulnerabilities, and identity concerns that emerge when AI agents take on roles inside organizations, and I pair that modeling with empirical analysis of how human–AI collaboration actually unfolds in practice.

Before the PhD I completed a Master of Digital Experience Innovation at the University of Waterloo (2024–2025) and a Bachelor of Information Science at the University of Toronto (2022–2024), preceded by studies in Mathematical Statistics at York University. I spent nearly a decade at Ontario Power Generation working in safety-critical nuclear environments — first in Instrumentation & Control at Pickering, then as a Data Analyst at Darlington leading audit-program planning and stakeholder-driven risk assessment. That background continues to shape how I think about requirements, accountability, and socio-technical risk.

## Current Research

Working with Professor Eric Yu on **Agent-Oriented Modeling for Enterprise AI and Sustainability**. The research combines two threads:

1. **Identity-sensitive requirements engineering.** Extending i\* with person-based, role-based, and relational identity dimensions to capture requirements for transparency, authority preservation, and competence development when AI agents take over professional tasks. Applied to a bank's AI talent-acquisition system in the CAiSE 2026 paper.
2. **Empirical human–AI collaboration analysis.** Analyzing 29,585 pull-request lifecycles across five AI coding tools (OpenAI, Copilot, Devin, Cursor, Claude Code) to characterize where tools sit on a Collaborator–Assistant spectrum (Cramér's V = 0.50), and to separate operational agency (who starts work) from governance authority (who merges). Reported in the AIware 2026 paper.

Methodologically, I work across goal modeling, viability analysis, label propagation, and large-N empirical analysis of software-engineering process data.
